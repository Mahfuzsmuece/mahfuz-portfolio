About

Building chip-scale optical routes for dense compute systems.

I am a graduate researcher at the University of Florida working on photonic ASIC components, ML-guided device design, cleanroom fabrication, chip testing, and packaging for next-generation optical interconnects.

My background bridges simulation, fabrication, metrology, and layout: FDTD/MODE/DEVICE workflows, semiconductor processing, SRAM layout in Cadence Virtuoso, and hands-on characterization of fabricated devices.

Research

Selected research directions

Low-loss vertical coupling, packaging-aware photonic layouts, and fabrication-informed design loops.

Aug 2024 - Present

Integrated Nano-Mirror Couplers and Photonic Wire Bonding

Developing scalable, low-loss vertical optical interconnects for chip-to-chip and wafer-scale packaging.

  • Optimized NMC geometry across multiple design variants.
  • Reduced coupling loss by 0.17 dB in design studies.
  • Built DOE and failure-analysis checklists for wafer-run execution.

Aug 2024 - Dec 2024

SRAM Design in Cadence Virtuoso

Designed and validated 45 nm FinFET standard cells and SRAM blocks with signoff-oriented layout checks.

  • Simulated INV, NAND, NOR, and SRAM behavior across PVT variations.
  • Completed I/O simulations, DRC, and LVS validation.
  • Focused on reliability, layout correctness, and device-level behavior.

Aug 2023 - May 2024

MOSFET, HBT, and Broad Area Laser Fabrication

Fabricated and characterized semiconductor devices through multi-step cleanroom processing and test preparation.

  • Used photolithography, deposition, etch, and diffusion processes.
  • Measured IV curves and compared sample-level variability.
  • Prepared device summaries for comparative evaluation.
